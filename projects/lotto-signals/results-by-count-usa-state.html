Lottery in Arizona

The Arizona State Lottery is a lottery run by the government of Arizona and is a member of the MUSL or the Multi-State Lottery Association. The goals of the lottery include generating money for the public benefit programs that have been initiated by the Arizona government. The proceeds from the lottery are used to fund public programs in four different divisions of the state; education, health and public welfare, economic and business development and environmental issues. The division that receives the most funding is education receiving about 55.5% of the lottery's revenue. It is followed by health and public welfare which receives 44.1%.

What are the Winning Records in Arizona State Lottery?

Many great prizes and jackpots have been awarded to the participants of the Arizona State Lottery. The Pick and Mega Millions are among the games that have produced the highest jackpot winners in the state of Arizona.

  • The highest amount of money that has been won in a Pick lottery up until now is $6.7 million which was won by a couple from Glendale in April of 2013
  • The highest amount of money that has been won in a Mega Millions lottery to date is $5 million which was won by a couple hailing from Sun City West in January of 2015.

What Taxes Applied on Arizona State Lottery Winnings?

A withholding tax of 25% is charged by the IRS on the winnings of a lottery player that exceeds $5,000. Since these winnings are also considered as income, he would have to pay income tax on it as well which can go up 35% depending on the winnings. Moreover, a state-tax is also applied on these winnings as well which in case of residents of Arizona is 5%. The tax rate rises to 6% for non-residents who have landed a jackpot.

All About Lottery in Arizona

History of Arizona State Lottery

Arizona was among the first states located on the west of the Mississippi to have a government lottery which was approved in the year 1980. In the early days, support for the lottery was barely what was required for its approval, but that has changed over the years and the results of a recent voting, which was done for extending the lottery in 2002, showed that 73% people were in favor of the lottery. The Arizona state lottery has been extended to 2035 according to a legislation that was passed in the year 2010.

Rules of Arizona State Lottery

Arizona state lottery applies all of the federal laws and general rules that are applied on all the public and state-run lotteries. Moreover, there are certain state-specific rules as well that the government of Arizona has come up with which have to be abided by the people participating in the lottery. Some of these rules are:

  • The minimum age of lottery players is not 18 but 21.
  • All tickets sales are final and non-refundable.
  • Winners have to collect their prizes within 180 days of the drawing.

Laws and Legalization of Arizona State Lottery

The Arizona state lottery was legalized in the year 1980 after a referendum. The thing that makes this lottery unique from the rest of the state lotteries is the fact that voting is done on a periodic basis to get the opinion of the residents regarding the games of the lottery that need to be continued. In addition to this, there are several other laws that govern the Arizona lottery. Some of these laws are:

  • The tickets for the Arizona lottery are not sold over the internet, mail or the telephone.
  • The number of consecutive draws that a person can play varies from game to game.
